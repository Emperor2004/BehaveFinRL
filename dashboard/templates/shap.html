KernelSHAP Explainer

Understanding Policy Explainability

Standard deep reinforcement learning agents are "black boxes"—their decision-making process is invisible. We employ **KernelSHAP** to attribute the model's target portfolio weight allocations (long/short/neutral) to its input features (technical indicators, macroeconomics, and market microstructure variables). This allows us to verify if the agent responds rationally to behavioral biases and macroeconomic regimes.

Bull Market Policy Attributes

In a Bull regime, the agent's objective is to capture upside trends by allocating high long weights. Features tracking moving average ratios, RSI momentum, and interest rates drive allocation decisions. Since the baseline loss aversion \(\lambda\) is relaxed (2.00), the agent responds strongly to positive price trends and targets fully invested weights.

Bear Market Policy Attributes

In a Bear regime, the agent becomes highly loss-averse (\(\lambda = 2.75\)). The policy is driven almost entirely by volatility indices (VIX), technical drawdown indicators, and macroeconomic risk metrics. The agent exhibits a heavy bias toward cash, liquidating positions rapidly at the first sign of price weakness to avoid steep psychological penalties.

Volatile Market Policy Attributes

In the intermediate Volatile/Transition state (\(\lambda = 2.40\)), the agent must balance trend-following with risk management. It reacts to yield curve spreads (T10Y2Y) and short-term volatility. The decision boundaries are highly complex, with the agent frequently transitioning to cash to avoid whipsaws.
